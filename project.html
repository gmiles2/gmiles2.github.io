Motivation and Aims of the Project

The United States experiences the highest rate of total drug overdoses in the world. Opioids in particular accounted for 74.6% (CDC, 2022) of all drug overdoses in 2020, with the ongoing opioid epidemic now killing around 70,000 Americans each year.

This project aims to provide a greater understanding of the magnitude of the opioid epidemic in the United States and to investigate how and why different areas of the US have been impacted to varying degrees.

Overdose Deaths in the US by Drug Type

The CDC WONDER database was used to separate opioid related deaths by different drug types. This was done by identifying the ICD-10 death codes relevant to each drug type and creating a custom dataset from the multiple cause of death database. This was exported as a text file and converted to a CSV using Excel.

The majority of the data in this project was collected from the CDC WONDER database. The database was hard to navigate at first but once I understood how the request form functioned the opioid overdose data could be broken down into many different categories and played a key role in my analysis. All of the datasets made using the database are linked below each visualisation.

Why Do Opioid Deaths Vary Between States?

In the final section of this project, three different economic indicators were plotted against the opioid death rate for each state for only 2019 to avoid any Covid-19 related noise. Data for the indicators were gathered using three separate web scrapers. For the pandas html reader scrapers the only challenges were cleaning up the formatting of the table headers and rows. For the house price data, Beautiful Soup was used as the pandas html reader would not read the data table. Beautiful Soup was more complicated to use at first but once the table element was identified in the raw html the data values and headers were extracted separately and then had to be cleaned with a series of string functions before being exported.

Unfortunately the correlations for income and house prices were weak and even show a counterintuitive result. The unemployment data is more successful but overall the metrics used did not reveal much of an answer to why the death rate differs across the US.

Project Conclusions

The charts produced provide some but not all of the answers to the aims outlined at the beginning. The US opioid crisis is by far the worst in the world and is growing deadlier each year. The northern-east coast of the US and specifically the states of West Virginia, Delaware, Maryland and Ohio are experiencing the highest death rates.

The section which aimed to answer why some states suffer more did not find any strong evidence. Therefore, there is motivation for some further research to be conducted. This could investigate the levels of social care, medical services and inequality rates within each state. It may also be interesting to make wider comparisons between the US and other countries to find out why this has become such a problem amongst Americans.
